🌲 Discover Olympia, Washington

🏞️ Parks

  • Priest Point Park: A waterfront park with scenic forested trails, beaches, picnic shelters, and breathtaking views of Puget Sound.
  • Heritage Park: Located next to the Capitol campus, featuring walking paths, fountains, and sweeping views of Capitol Lake and downtown Olympia.
  • Squaxin Park (formerly Priest Point East): A natural space with native plant gardens, trails, and interpretive displays.
  • Yauger Park: Popular for its sports fields, skate park, playgrounds, and large rain garden system.

🏫 Schools

Olympia is served by the well-respected Olympia School District:

  • Olympia High School and Capital High School: Offering a variety of academic programs and extracurricular activities.
  • Reeves, Marshall, and Washington Middle Schools: Known for strong academic support and community involvement.
  • Numerous elementary schools including Lincoln, Garfield, and Centennial Elementary provide a solid educational foundation.

The city is also home to The Evergreen State College, offering liberal arts programs with an emphasis on interdisciplinary learning.

🎯 Local Attractions

  • Washington State Capitol Campus: Take a guided tour of the historic Capitol Building and its surrounding gardens and monuments.
  • Olympia Farmers Market: A vibrant hub of local produce, crafts, and live entertainment, open year-round.
  • Hands On Children’s Museum: An award-winning, interactive museum designed to engage kids and families in creative play.
  • Capitol Lake & Boardwalk: Perfect for a stroll, jog, or birdwatching with views of the Capitol and Olympic Mountains.

Olympia offers a perfect blend of government, culture, education, and natural beauty — all in one walkable and welcoming community.
